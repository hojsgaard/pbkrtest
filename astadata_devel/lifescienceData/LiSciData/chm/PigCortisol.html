PigCortisol(dataRep)R Documentation

Diurnal rythm of cortisol in pigs on two different treatments

Description

In an experiment investigating the effect of stress on pigs (control vs. intermittent stress), the cortisol level in the blood was sampled every half hour from 12 to 36 hours after implantation of a catheter. Cortisol is a hormone involved in the stress response. The pigs were blocked according to litter.

Usage

data(PigCortisol)

Format

A data frame with 900 observations on the following 7 variables.

time
Time in hours post operation, a numeric vector
cortisol
The cortisol level, a numeric vector
sint
sin(time*2*pi/24), a numeric vector
cost
cos(time*2*pi/24), a numeric vector
pig
The ID of the pig, a factor with a level for each pig
litter
The Litter of the pig, a factor with levels 1,...,8
treat
The treatment of the pig, a factor with levels 1 2

Details

Source

Jensen, K.H., Pedersen, L.J., Heller, K.E., Ladewig, J. and Jørgensen, E. (1996). Intermittent Stress in pigs: effects on behavior, pituarity-adrenocortical axis, growth and gastric ulceration. Physiology & Behaviour, (59), p 741-748,
